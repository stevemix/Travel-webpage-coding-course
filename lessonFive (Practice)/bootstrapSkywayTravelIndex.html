Paris - "The City Of Light"

Welcome To Paris!

Paris, France's capital, is a major European city and a global center for art, fashion, gastronomy and culture. Its 19th-century cityscape is crisscrossed by wide boulevards and the River Seine. .

Beyond such landmarks as the Eiffel Tower and the 12th-century, Gothic Notre-Dame cathedral, the city is known for its cafe culture and designer boutiques along the Rue du Faubourg Saint-Honoré.

Parisian cafés serve as a center of social and culinary life in Paris. They have existed since the 17th century, and serve as the meeting place, neighborhood hub, a rendez-vous spot and a place to relax.

Rome - "The Eternal City"

Check Out Rome!

Rome is the capital city and a special comune of Italy, as well as the capital of the Lazio region. The city has been a major human settlement for almost three millennia. .

With 2,860,009 residents in 1,285 km², it is also the country's most populated comune. you'll find the smallest country in the world in Rome; Vatican City, the centre of the Christian Faith.

Parks and nature reserves cover a large area and the city has one of the largest areas of green space among European capitals. This is represented by the large number of villas and landscaped gardens.

New York - "The City That Never Sleeps!"

Visit The Big Apple!

New York City comprises of 5 boroughs sitting where the Hudson River meets the Atlantic Ocean. At its core is Manhattan, a densely populated borough that’s one of the world’s major commercial, financial and cultural centers.

Its iconic sites include skyscrapers such as the Empire State Building, the world's tallest building from 1931 to 1970 and the sprawling Central Park. The cities Entertainment hub is the Broadway theatres staged in neon-lit Times Square..

The Statue of Liberty. completed in October 1886 on Liberty Island in New York Harbor is a symbol of the United States and its ideals of freedom, democracy, and opportunity. Proudly standing to greet you as you enter by sea.
